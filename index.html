Section 01 · The System

How to use this bible.

This document is not a description. It is a reference engine. You don't read it once and put it away — you keep it open every time you open Midjourney, Sora, or Higgsfield. Every prompt you write starts with copy-pasting from here.

Step 01

Generate the master portrait once

For each of the ten kids, run the Anchor Prompt from their profile until you get a portrait you genuinely love. This is your North Star image. Save it as [name]_master.png.

Step 02

Build expression & action sheets

Pass the master portrait as a reference image (Midjourney --cref, Sora image-to-image, or Higgsfield character lock) and run the eight expression prompts and eight action prompts. You now have a 17-image kit per character.

Step 03

Reference, don't redescribe

For every keyframe in every video, your prompt format is: "[World Boilerplate] + [Shot description] + [reference: master.png]". Never describe the character in words and hope. The reference image does the heavy lifting.

Step 04

Quality gate every frame

Before animating any keyframe, hold it next to the master portrait. Hair colour, eye colour, face shape, signature outfit — all must match. If anything has drifted, regenerate. One off-model frame breaks immersion for an entire video.

Section 02 · World Rules

The universal style anchor.

Before any individual character: the rules of the world they all live in. Lighting, palette, camera, line quality. Lock these once and every shot — even shots without a character — will read as Konfident Kids.

★ World Boilerplate · paste at top of every image promptrequired
Pixar-style premium 3D family animation, polished cinematic lighting with warm soft key and gentle yellow rim light, golden-hour ambience, vibrant saturated palette anchored on KonfidentKids navy (#0B1A3A) and golden yellow (#FFD23F) with cream highlights, rounded child-friendly proportions, large expressive eyes, soft skin shading, clean rendering with no harsh edges, painterly hand-finished textures, child-safe and welcoming, joyful uplifting mood, 16:9 cinematic frame, depth of field with subtle bokeh, premium streaming family movie quality.
⊘ Universal Negative Prompt · always exclude

scary, dark, gritty, photorealistic adult, harsh shadows, sexualised, weapons, violence, low quality, deformed, extra fingers, extra limbs, blurry faces, distorted proportions, generic anime style, flat 2D cartoon, hyperrealistic skin pores, ageing/wrinkles, sad or distressed children, gloomy palette, washed-out colours, oversaturated neon, AI-generated artefacts, watermarks, text overlays, brand logos other than KonfidentKids, copyrighted character likenesses (Pixar/Disney/DreamWorks IP).

★ Palette

Navy & golden yellow lead

  • Primary: navy #0B1A3A + yellow #FFD23F
  • Cream/warm white #FFF8E7 for skies, highlights
  • Coral, teal, mint, soft pink as accent costume colours
  • No neons, no greys, no muddy browns in environments
★ Lighting

Golden hour, always

  • Soft warm key light (45° upper-front)
  • Gentle yellow rim light from behind for separation
  • Lifted shadows — no deep blacks on faces
  • Subtle dust motes or light particles for magic moments
★ Camera

Mostly eye-level, sometimes hero

  • Default: eye-level shot at child height (~1.2m)
  • Empowerment beats: slight low-angle (hero shot)
  • Discovery beats: gentle push-in (3–6 second clip)
  • Group shots: wide 16:9, ensemble in soft arc
★ Backgrounds

Rounded shapes, soft gradients

  • Rolling green meadows, warm pine forests
  • Bright cumulus clouds, soft sky gradients
  • Stylised playgrounds, school classrooms, bedrooms
  • Never sharp/angular geometry — everything rounded
★ Proportions

Child-friendly, never adult

  • 4–4.5 head body ratio (proper kid proportions)
  • Large eyes (1/4 of face vertical), small nose
  • Soft round cheeks, no defined jawline
  • Hands slightly oversized for expression
★ Brand Anchors

Repeatable visual identity

  • Kai's white star is the singular brand emblem
  • Yellow accents always present somewhere in frame
  • Outdoor scenes default to meadow / forest setting
  • Group ensemble matches Twitter header arrangement

★ Reference Image Strategy

Generative AI cannot remember your character between sessions. Every prompt is a fresh roll of the dice. The fix is to always pass a locked reference image alongside your prompt. In Midjourney, this is the --cref [URL] --cw 100 flag (cw = character weight, 100 = maximum). In Sora, drop the master portrait into the image-input slot. In Higgsfield, use the character lock feature. Without a reference, you are gambling. With one, you are directing.

Section 07 · Quality Gate

Before you animate anything.

A keyframe that's slightly off-model is worse than a keyframe you regenerate. One drifted hair colour or missing pair of glasses ripples across every shot in the song. Run this checklist on every keyframe before it leaves the keyframes folder.

  • ★ Is the hair colour, length and style identical to the master portrait?
  • ★ Are the eyes the right colour? (Nova hazel, Ollie blue, Kai brown, Lily light brown, Finn blue, Zane pale blue, Maya/Jayden/Leo brown, Ruby blue.)
  • ★ Is the signature outfit present? (Kai's red star, Maya's yellow headband, Ollie's suspenders & cap, Jayden's backwards cap, Ruby's tablet, Lily's cloud tee, Leo's suspenders, Nova's coral hoodie, Finn's blue zip hoodie, Zane's grey tee.)
  • ★ Is the signature accessory in frame? (Glasses on Nova and Ollie are non-negotiable.)
  • ★ Are skin tones consistent? (Jayden deep brown, Maya/Leo medium brown, all others fair to peachy.)
  • ★ Are proportions child-like? (4–4.5 head body ratio. No tall stretched bodies. No wrinkles or adult features.)
  • ★ Is the lighting golden hour with warm yellow rim light? (No grey, blue, or harsh light without an emotional reason.)
  • ★ Does the background follow world rules? (Rounded shapes, soft gradients, navy + yellow accents.)
  • ★ No extra fingers, melted hands, or floating objects? (Hands and small props are where AI fails most often.)
  • ★ Side-by-side test: hold the keyframe next to the master portrait. Does it pass the "same kid" sniff test in under three seconds?
★

The single rule that makes everything else work

Generative AI does not remember your characters. Every time you open a fresh chat, fresh tab, or fresh tool — your cast resets to nothing. The Character Bible exists to overwrite that reset every single time. Reference the master portrait. Always. Every prompt. No exceptions. One discipline. Forty videos. The same ten kids.
